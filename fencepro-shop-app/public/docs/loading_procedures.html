Loading

Vehicle & Trailer Safety

Goal: Load material securely and safely. Material is balanced and easy to unload at the site.

💡 Why This Matters

A badly loaded trailer is a safety hazard. Improper weight distribution causes trailer sway at highway speeds. Loose material can shift or fall off. Loading it right the first time protects people, product, and the schedule.

🚛 Loading Rules & Safety

  1. Safety Checks: Trailer brake check, Tire pressure check.
    • Reason: Brakes and tires are non-negotiable. A brake failure with 3,000+ lbs of material is catastrophic.
  2. Weight Distribution:
    • 80-90% of weight over the axle(s).
    • 10% - 20% on the tongue. No significant weight behind the axle.
    • Tongue down is okay. Never tongue up.
    • Reason: Tongue-heavy is stable; tongue-light causes dangerous fish-tailing.
  3. Placement & Strapping:
    • Parallel deck from wheels to front of trailer.
    • Load in reverse order of use (e.g. posts first, pickets last).
    • Two straps per pallet. All ratchets on same side.
✅ Expected Outcome

Every trailer leaves the yard road-safe and load-balanced. Zero material damage in transit and zero safety incidents on the road.
