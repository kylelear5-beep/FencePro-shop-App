Material Receiving SOP

Running Tasks & Receiving

Warehouse Operations

Goal: All material is received for accounting and stored within 24 hours of delivery.

💡 Why This Matters

Receiving is the front door of every job. If material isn’t checked in accurately, accounting can’t reconcile costs, the office can’t schedule installations, and crews show up to a job site missing what they need. Every hour material sits un-received increases the risk of lost, damaged, or misattributed inventory.

Running Tasks: Receiving hours are 9am to 12pm. Receive any material that comes in. Inspect when it arrives. If obviously damaged OR the wrong material, call supplier directly and don’t take off from truck.

Receiving Checklist

  1. Find PO: Find purchase order for the delivery. If none, contact buyer.
    • Reason: The PO is our single source of truth for what we actually ordered.
  2. Match PO: Match purchase order to delivery slip. If issue, contact vendor.
    • Reason: Vendors make mistakes — wrong quantities, substituted items, partial shipments.
  3. Truck Inspection: On flat bed, in box truck, or “delivered good”.
    • Good: All material matches delivery slip and is not broken. Put in its location.
    • Wrong material: Contact vendor asap.
    • Broken material: Take photos, write down damage on BOL, contact vendor asap.
  4. Unload & Load: Unload, perform further inspection if needed. For stock material, put in appropriate bay. For job lot material, put in free location or bay for staging.
  5. Sign Off:
    • Received: Initial PO with Date, initials, ‘all good’. Staple delivery slip/BOL to back, put in ‘received folder’.
    • Received error: Describe error on PO, put in Error folder.
✅ Expected Outcome

Every delivery is verified against its PO, inspected for damage, stored in the correct location, and signed off within 24 hours.
