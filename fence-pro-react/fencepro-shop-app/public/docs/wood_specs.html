Wood Fence Specifications

Wood Styles & Materials

Common Fence Styles

Privacy (Stockade / Board-on-Board)

Designed for maximum seclusion. Board-on-board uses overlapping boards for a strong, zero-gap barrier.

Shadowbox

Alternating boards on each side. Offers privacy + air circulation. Looks identical on both sides.

Picket

Traditional decorative boundary. Typically 4ft tall. Defines space without blocking view.

Split-Rail

Rustic look for large properties or livestock. Consists of horizontal rails fitted into posts.

Wood Species Reference

Pressure-Treated Pine

Cost-effective, chemically treated to resist rot. Popular for Southern locations. Can be stained/painted.

Cedar (Western Red)

Naturally rot/insect resistant due to oils. Deep color and pleasant aroma. Silver-gray patina over time.

Redwood

Premium choice. Exceptional natural beauty and durability. High tannin content resists decay.

Cypress

High water resistance. Stable and unlikely to warp. Perfect for humid climates.
