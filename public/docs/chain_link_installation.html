4 Adding Fittings to Terminal Posts
1

After concrete footings have been allowed to sufficiently harden, slip the rail end bands (brace bands) and tension bands onto the terminal posts. (Refer to parts list for the description and quantity of fittings that are required for various post types and heights.) The long flat surface of the tension band should face toward the outside of the fence. Fig. 5

NOTE: Take care not to spread or distort the fittings.
2

Apply all terminal post caps.

Fitting Layout — End & Gate Post Fig. 5

Top to bottom: Terminal Post Cap → Rail End Band (Brace Band) → Tension Bands → Rail End Band (Brace Band) for Tension Wire (bottom).

Fitting Layout — Corner Post Fig. 5

Same layout but duplicated on each side of the corner. Double the number of tension bands.

Terrain Adjustments

Terraced Ground Fig. 6

Corner post assembly is used at point A to allow fabric to follow terraced contour of ground. The fence steps down in sections, with each section remaining level.

Very Uneven Ground Fig. 7

Corner post assembly is used at points A and B when ground rises or drops more than 15″ per 100 linear feet. This allows the fabric and top rail to angle with the slope while maintaining proper tension.

5 Installing Top Rails
1

Place line post top (loop cap) on the top of each line post. The off-set round side should be toward the outside of the fence. Fig. 8

2

Insert one length of top rail through the line post top (loop cap) closest to a terminal post. Slip rail end (cup) onto the end of the top rail and attach it to a terminal post by using a rail end band (brace band). Secure by using a 5/16″ × 1-1/4″ carriage bolt with the head to the outside of the fence. Fig. 9

3

Continue by forcing lengths of swedge-end top rail together through the line post tops (loop caps). (If swedge-end top rail is not used, join lengths together with top rail sleeves.) Fig. 10

4

Upon reaching the next terminal post, measure carefully and cut the top rail to fit tightly between the last length of top rail and the rail end (cup). Fasten rail end (cup) to rail end band (brace band) on the terminal post. Secure in place with a 5/16″ × 1-1/4″ carriage bolt. Fig. 11

6 Adding Tension Wire (Optional)
💡 Tension wire runs along the bottom of the fence to prevent the fabric from being pushed up or pulled out. While optional, it is highly recommended for security and durability.
1

Wrap tension wire once around bottom rail end band (brace band) carriage bolt. Using pliers, twist several times to secure. Fig. 12

Tension wire should run along the same side of the posts as the fabric.

2

Apply tension wire clips (hog rings) no more than 24″ apart (or 12″ recommended per shop standard) for securing the wire to the chain-link fabric.

Tension Wire – Quick Reference

Wire length: Same linear footage as fence, minus gate openings.

Clip spacing: Every 24″ maximum (12″ recommended per shop standard).

Attachment: Wrap once around carriage bolt at bottom rail end band (brace band), twist to secure at each terminal post.
